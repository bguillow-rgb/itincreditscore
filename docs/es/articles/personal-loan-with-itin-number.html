Personal Loan With an ITIN Number (2026 Guide)

Getting a personal loan when you don’t have a Social Security number can feel like running into a wall — but the wall has more doors than most people realize. Lenders are legally allowed to issue personal loans to ITIN holders, and a growing number actively do. The key is knowing which lenders to approach, what they look at instead of (or alongside) a credit score, and how your ITIN credit profile affects the rate you’ll pay.

Is it actually possible to get a personal loan with just an ITIN?

A question we hear often: whether the whole thing is even legal before worrying about rates or lenders.

The short answer is yes, and it’s entirely legal. From a legal standpoint, no federal law prohibits lenders from issuing personal loans to borrowers without Social Security numbers. Lenders need a way to identify borrowers and verify tax reporting obligations, and an ITIN satisfies that requirement. Whether a lender accepts an ITIN is a business decision, not a legal limitation.

That framing matters — it means you’re not asking for a special favor or workaround. You’re simply applying through lenders that have chosen to serve borrowers like you. Most major national banks do not accept ITIN for personal loans. Credit unions, online lenders, and specialty finance companies that offer programs specifically for ITIN borrowers are more realistic options.

Once you know where to look, the process closely mirrors a standard personal loan application. The difference is documentation — more on that below.

A credit union can be a good choice for ITIN holders because they often provide competitive rates and personalized service — ITIN lending programs allow credit unions to fulfill their mission of people helping people. You can use an ITIN to apply for personal loans, auto loans, and even mortgages.

What credit score do lenders want, and what if I don’t have one yet?

This one comes up a lot — especially from readers who have an ITIN but haven’t had a chance to build U.S. credit history yet.

Credit requirements vary by lender. Some ITIN loan programs require traditional credit scores, often starting around 580. Others rely on alternative credit documentation, such as rental payment history or utility bills. Higher is always better: credit strength plays a significant role in pricing — borrowers with scores above 680 and stable income generally receive better rates than those with thin credit files or limited repayment history.

If you don’t yet have a U.S. credit score, you’re not necessarily locked out. Many ITIN-friendly lenders use what the industry calls “alternative credit” — documented proof that you pay recurring obligations on time. Credit can be evaluated using traditional or alternative methods. Traditional credit relies on a score from a major credit bureau, often with a minimum of around 620. Alternative credit may include documented payment history for rent, utilities, phone bills, insurance, or other recurring obligations.

What interest rates and loan amounts should I expect?

Readers frequently ask whether ITIN personal loans are predatory by nature — they’re not, but you do pay more than a borrower with a long SSN-based credit history.

Interest rates on ITIN personal loans are higher than comparable SSN-based loans. Typical APRs range from roughly 12% to 36%, with some specialty lenders charging more. The wide range reflects how heavily your credit profile matters: a 700+ score and two years of stable income can land you near the bottom of that range, while a thin file or recent negative marks will push you toward the top.

On loan size, without an SSN and with a thin or no U.S. credit file, most lenders cap loan amounts between $1,000 and $25,000, with many lenders capping at $10,000–$15,000. Alliant Credit Union, for example, offers ITIN borrowers $1,000–$100,000 with a repayment period of 12–60 months, and a minimum APR of 8.74%. That upper range is uncommon, however — it reflects a well-qualified member with an established credit profile at that specific institution.

One practical note: most ITIN personal loans are unsecured. Most ITIN personal loans are unsecured, meaning no collateral is required. Approval is based on income, credit profile, and repayment capacity rather than pledged assets — unsecured ITIN loans allow borrowers to receive funds without risking specific property. If you’re comfortable putting up collateral (a savings account, for example), a secured personal loan often comes with a meaningfully lower rate.

What documents do I need to apply?

Expect lenders to ask for documents in three categories: identity, income, and address. Having everything organized before you apply is the single most effective way to avoid delays — inconsistent information or missing documents is the fastest way to slow down or lose approval for your loan.

Identity documents:

  • Your IRS ITIN assignment letter (CP565) or renewal confirmation (CP567)
  • A current foreign passport or consular identification card
  • Some lenders also accept a state-issued ID that doesn’t require an SSN

Income documents:

  • Recent pay stubs (W-2 employees) or 12–24 months of bank statements (self-employed)
  • Two most recent federal tax returns filed with your ITIN
  • For self-employed borrowers: profit-and-loss statements may be accepted in place of tax returns

Residency / address proof:

  • Utility bill, lease agreement, or bank statement showing your U.S. address
  • Some lenders require proof of continuous U.S. residency for 1–2 years

Two years of continuous employment — either with the same employer or in the same line of work — is the usual requirement for most lenders. Self-employed borrowers may also qualify, provided they can show at least two years of self-employment history and submit relevant bank statements or tax returns.

How does my immigration status affect my application?

A question we hear often, and understandably — many ITIN holders are uncertain whether their visa status or documentation situation will disqualify them outright.

The reassuring reality is that immigration status and lending eligibility are legally separate matters. ITINs are issued to a range of individuals, including certain visa holders, non-resident aliens with U.S. income, and dependents who are required to file U.S. tax returns but do not qualify for Social Security numbers. Lenders assess income stability, credit history, and repayment capacity rather than visa category.

That said, individual lenders set their own restrictions. Some ITIN loan lenders extend personal loans to ITIN holders regardless of immigration status, while others apply additional restrictions. What matters most to lenders is a borrower’s ability to repay the loan, supported by income documentation and repayment history. If one lender declines you citing status concerns, that’s a lender-specific policy — not a universal barrier. Move to the next option on your list.

How do I avoid predatory lenders targeting ITIN borrowers?

Because the pool of ITIN-friendly lenders is smaller than the mainstream market, bad actors have an easier time finding vulnerable borrowers. Knowing the red flags protects you.

ITIN borrowers are often targeted by predatory lenders who take advantage of limited lending options and gaps in borrower knowledge — knowing what to look for makes it easier to avoid loans that carry high costs or abusive terms.

Specific warning signs to watch for:

  • Guaranteed approval claims. No legitimate lender guarantees approval without reviewing your application — guaranteed approval usually means guaranteed bad terms.
  • No income verification. Responsible ITIN loan lenders verify your ability to repay. Skipping this step indicates the lender doesn’t care if you can afford the loan.
  • Upfront fees before funding. Legitimate lenders deduct origination fees from loan proceeds — they never collect fees before the loan is disbursed.
  • Too many hard inquiries at once. Multiple hard credit inquiries can lower scores and signal risk — research lenders carefully and limit formal applications to a small number of well-matched options.

For rate comparison without hard inquiries, marketplace tools like MoneyLion and LendingTree let you see prequalified offers before you formally apply.

Will a personal loan with an ITIN help me build credit toward bigger goals?

This is the right long-game question. Personal loans help your credit profile in two specific ways: they add to your payment history (the largest factor in most scoring models, accounting for roughly 35%) and they diversify your credit mix beyond credit cards alone.

The critical requirement is that the lender reports your payments to all three major bureaus. Ask any lender directly before applying: “Do you report monthly payment activity to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion?”

Once you have a functioning credit score, larger doors open quickly. A solid ITIN credit file can eventually qualify you for a car loan with your ITIN number, and over time, even an ITIN mortgage. According to a commonly cited industry benchmark, it takes about three to six months of consistent, reported account activity to generate your first credit score — credit bureaus need enough data to calculate a score, which requires at least one active account reporting for several months. A personal loan reporting each month accelerates that timeline, especially when combined with a secured credit card.

Preguntas frecuentes

Can I get a personal loan with only an ITIN and no credit history?

Some lenders — especially credit unions and community development financial institutions (CDFIs) — accept alternative credit documentation such as rent receipts, utility bills, and phone payment history in place of a traditional credit score. You'll likely face higher rates and smaller loan limits, but it is possible.

What credit score do I need for a personal loan with an ITIN?

Requirements vary widely. Some ITIN loan programs start around 580, while others require 620–660 or higher. Borrowers above 680 with stable income generally get the most competitive rates. If you have no U.S. credit score yet, focus on building one first using a secured card or credit-builder loan.

Will getting a personal loan with my ITIN help my credit score?

Yes — as long as the lender reports to the major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). An installment loan adds a new account type to your credit mix and builds payment history, both of which can meaningfully improve your score over time.

Do major banks like Wells Fargo or Chase accept ITINs for personal loans?

A small number of major banks accept ITINs for personal loans, but only if you already have an established banking relationship with them. Chase, for example, generally requires an SSN. Wells Fargo may accept ITINs for existing account holders. Credit unions and specialty online lenders are more reliably ITIN-friendly.

Is it legal for lenders to give personal loans to ITIN holders?

Absolutely. No federal law prohibits a lender from issuing a personal loan to someone without a Social Security number. An ITIN satisfies the lender's need to identify a borrower and verify tax-reporting obligations. Whether to accept an ITIN is each lender's individual business decision, not a legal restriction.

How much can I borrow with a personal loan using an ITIN?

Most ITIN-friendly lenders cap personal loans between $1,000 and $25,000, with many limiting first-time borrowers to $10,000–$15,000. Borrowers with stronger credit profiles and stable income documented over two or more years can often qualify for higher amounts.
