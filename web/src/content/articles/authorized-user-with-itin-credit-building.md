Becoming an authorized user is one of the fastest ways for an ITIN holder to get a U.S. credit score onto the books. You inherit the primary cardholder's account history the moment the issuer reports you to the bureaus, which can mean jumping from credit-invisible to scoreable in a single billing cycle. But a few details determine whether this strategy works well or backfires entirely.

## Can an ITIN holder actually be added as an authorized user?

*A question we hear often:* many readers assume they need a Social Security Number before any issuer will add them to an account. That is not accurate.

Most issuers require the authorized user's full legal name and date of birth. Many also ask for a Social Security number or ITIN, though some only require a name. In practice, your ITIN is sufficient at most major institutions, and at some you do not even need to provide it. Chase, American Express, Bank of America, and Capital One all report authorized user activity to the credit bureaus. Those are four of the most widely held cards in the country, so the odds are good that someone in your family or trusted circle carries one of them.

The most important step before anything else: call the issuer and confirm they report authorized user accounts to all three bureaus (Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax). Being an authorized user building credit first depends on whether the card issuer reports the authorized user's information to the credit bureaus. Credit-scoring companies calculate scores based only on information on your credit reports, so the card account must appear on your reports for it to affect your credit scores. If the account does not appear, it will not impact your credit history at all.

## How does authorized user status affect my credit score as an ITIN holder?

As an authorized user, you benefit from the account's entire history, including the length of time it has been open, the payment record, and the credit limit. If the primary cardholder has had the card for 10 years with perfect payments, the authorized user gets all of that history on their report. This can be especially powerful for someone with no credit history at all. A single authorized user account with a long history of on-time payments can establish a credit score quickly.

For ITIN holders starting from zero, that account age boost matters a lot. An ITIN credit score can build credit history length because bureaus track account age and activity regardless of whether an SSN or ITIN is used. Credit history length is one key factor in FICO and VantageScore calculations, and the longer an account remains current, the greater the positive impact on the score.

The bureaus identify your file using your name, date of birth, and address, not the ITIN number itself. Credit bureaus, Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax, match reported accounts to an individual using identification elements including full name, date of birth, and current address. So even if the issuer only collects your name when adding you, the bureau can still tie the account to your existing credit file as long as that identifying information matches.

According to a 2026 SSRN study by Cookson, Guttman-Kenney, and Mullins, some immigrants access credit using an ITIN before receiving an SSN because it is possible to build a credit file without a Social Security number. Authorized user accounts are one of the primary paths those immigrants use to get there faster.

## What should I look for in a primary cardholder before asking?

*This one comes up a lot:* the benefit you receive depends almost entirely on the primary cardholder's behavior, not yours.

When looking for someone to add you as an authorized user, be sure that person pays the account as agreed, has had the account open for a while, and maintains a low credit utilization ratio. If so, the individual's good credit habits may positively impact your credit score. Otherwise, becoming an authorized user could hurt your credit.

Here is a simple checklist to use before asking:

- **Payment history:** Zero late payments, ideally for at least 12-24 months.
- **Credit utilization:** Balance consistently below 30% of the credit limit.
- **Account age:** The older the account, the more history transfers to your report.
- **Bureau reporting:** Confirm the issuer reports AU accounts to all three bureaus.
- **Your own monitoring:** Check your credit report 30-60 days after being added to verify the account actually appeared.

It is important to note that while authorized user status may help your credit, it can also negatively impact your score if the primary cardholder does not manage credit responsibly. For example, your credit score may take a hit if they have bad credit or pay their credit card bills late. Unfortunately, you do not have control of the primary cardholder's actions and may face a lower credit score as a result of poor decisions they make.

## How does this compare to other credit-building tools available to ITIN holders?

*Readers frequently ask* whether authorized user status is enough on its own, or whether it needs to be combined with something else.

Honestly, AU status is a powerful accelerator, not a complete substitute for accounts in your own name. Lenders reviewing your profile for a loan or a lease will want to see that you have managed credit independently, not only borrowed someone else's track record.

| Strategy | Requires SSN? | Reports to All 3 Bureaus? | Builds Your Own History? | Speed to First Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Authorized user | Usually no | Depends on issuer | No (borrowed history) | 1-2 billing cycles |
| Secured credit card | No (ITIN accepted widely) | Most do | Yes | 3-6 months |
| Credit-builder loan | No (ITIN accepted) | Yes, when lender reports to all 3 | Yes | 6-12 months |
| Rent reporting service | No | Varies by service | Yes | 1-3 months |

For ITIN holders starting from scratch, a practical combination is: get added as an authorized user to jumpstart a score, then open a [credit-builder loan with your ITIN](/credit-builder-loan-with-itin) at the same time to begin building history that is 100% yours. Once your score crosses 600-620, you can move to a secured card that reports to all three bureaus.

According to Experian's February 2026 white paper authored by Theresa Nguyen, 76.9% of ITIN holders remained current on trades after 12 months, a rate 15% higher than SSN consumers. That data point matters here: if you are disciplined about the accounts you do control, your credit profile will grow steadily even if the AU account ever disappears.

## What happens to my credit score if I am removed as an authorized user?

This is a risk worth understanding before you rely on AU status too heavily. If the primary cardholder closes their account, removes you, or the account turns negative, that account can disappear from your credit report entirely.

Being an authorized user may help you build credit. It first depends on whether the card issuer reports the authorized user's information to the credit bureaus. By the same logic, if the issuer stops reporting or the relationship ends, the positive history tied to that account may no longer appear in your file.

This is why you should use the window that AU status gives you to open at least one account in your own name. Your goal is to move from borrowed history to an independent credit profile. Check your report regularly using the steps in our guide on [how to get your credit report with an ITIN](/how-to-check-credit-score-with-itin-number). Catching errors or changes early lets you respond before your score takes a hit.

## Is there a scam I should watch out for here?

*A question we hear often*, and it deserves a direct answer.

The Federal Trade Commission warns of a related practice called tradeline renting or credit piggybacking. This is where companies sign you up as an authorized user on a stranger's credit in exchange for a fee. The FTC says such practices can be illegal and cannot guarantee any benefit to your credit scores.

Only ask to be added as an authorized user by someone you know personally and trust. Paying a third-party service to place you on a stranger's account is something the FTC has explicitly flagged, and it can result in no benefit or, worse, a fraud flag on your file. The legitimate path is simpler: a family member or close friend with a well-managed account adds you directly through their issuer's app or customer service line.

## How do I verify the authorized user account actually landed on my credit report?

The account may appear on your credit reports within one billing cycle, which is often 30 days. You might see changes to your credit scores soon after.

To confirm it landed correctly:

1. Wait 30-45 days after the primary cardholder adds you.
2. Request your credit report from each bureau. As an ITIN holder, you may need to submit your request by mail or phone to Experian, TransUnion, and Equifax directly, since you must enter your Social Security Number or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number to receive a free credit report through AnnualCreditReport.com.
3. Look for the account under your open accounts section. Verify the payment history shown is clean and that the credit limit is listed accurately.
4. If the account does not appear after 60 days, contact the issuer to confirm they actually submitted your information to the bureaus and that they have your correct identifying details on file.

If you spot an error, such as a late payment that is not yours or a wrong credit limit, you have the right to dispute it. Our guide on [how to dispute credit report errors with an ITIN](/how-to-dispute-credit-report-errors-with-itin) walks through the exact steps under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, which protects ITIN holders the same as SSN holders.
